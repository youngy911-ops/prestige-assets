Presenter Script

Slide 1 — Title

"Thanks for the time. I want to show you something I've built that I think changes how we do asset book-ins. It's live, it's working, and I've been using it on real jobs. I'll keep this short — five minutes of slides, then I'd rather show you the actual tool."

Slide 2 — The Problem

"Here's what currently happens when we book in an excavator or a prime mover. We take photos on-site, which is fine. But then we're manually cross-referencing websites to get the right specs, finding the exact Salesforce field order, and writing a description that has to follow our formatting rules exactly — no dot points, right footer, correct subtype layout. That's 5–15 minutes per asset. Multiply that across a full sale — 50, 100 lots — and it adds up fast. And the consistency isn't always there either, especially across different staff or asset types they don't book in often."

Slide 3 — The Solution

"The app flips that process. Staff upload the build plate photos directly from their phone. GPT-4o reads the plate and extracts every relevant Salesforce field — VIN, make, model, year, engine details, weight ratings, hours — and it uses its own knowledge to fill in spec fields it can infer from the model. The staff member reviews the AI's work, fixes anything wrong, and hits confirm. They get a ready-to-paste Salesforce fields block and a correctly formatted description. The whole thing takes about two minutes."

Slide 4 — What It Covers

"It's not just trucks and excavators. We cover all eight asset types with every Salesforce subtype aligned exactly to the SF taxonomy — the same choices you'd pick in the dropdown. Agriculture, Forklift, Caravan all have subtype selectors now. And critically, every subtype has its own description template. An EWP description looks different to a Tipper, a Telehandler looks different to a Walkie Stacker. The AI knows the difference and formats accordingly."

Slide 5 — Who Can Use It

"One thing I want to flag — this isn't just for me. Anyone can use it. You don't need to know the Salesforce field order for a prime mover or what a crawler tractor description is supposed to look like. The app handles all of that. A BDM can book in an asset on-site without handing it off. A new staff member gets it right without anyone showing them the ropes. Valuations team are done before they leave the yard.

And think about on-site auctions. Right now there's always a lag — photos get taken and then the data entry happens later. With this, you walk the yard and you're done. By the time you're back at the office the book-ins are already in Salesforce. Same for large general goods lots — instead of sitting there writing individual descriptions for 40 items, you're moving through them on your phone. It changes the pace of how we turn a job around."

Slide 6 — Description Quality

"The descriptions have to follow our format rules exactly, every time — not just when I'm the one writing them. The footer is enforced by the app. It cannot output the wrong variant. It never writes TBC for a spec it doesn't know — it just omits it. Every subtype has its own template, so a concrete agitator has drum capacity and mix details, an EWP has boom length and platform height. The output is editable before you copy it, but the starting point is already correct."

Slide 7 — Live Demo

"Let me just show you. [Open app] This is what staff see. They pick the asset type — I'll pick Truck, Tipper. Upload photos from the camera roll. [Upload] The AI runs for about 15 seconds. [Wait] And here are the extracted fields — make, model, VIN from the plate, year, engine details. The yellow ones are fields it's less confident about — we review those. [Fix one if needed] Here's the Salesforce fields output, ready to paste. And then [tap Generate Description] here's the description. Tipper format, correct footer. [Show copy button] That's it."

Slide 8 — Not a Prototype

"This isn't a demo script I threw together — it's production software with 365 automated tests. Every description rule, every field extraction path, every Salesforce subtype is tested. The AI calls are all server-side so no asset data is exposed client-side. And adding a new asset type when Slattery adds a new category is hours, not weeks."

Slide 9 — What's Next

"The one thing that would make this a lot more powerful is Salesforce API access. Right now the app gives you the output ready to paste — that's already saving real time. But if IT can set up a Connected App, the copy-paste step goes away entirely. You confirm the AI's work and the app writes straight into Salesforce. I can build that side of it, I just need IT to approve it. Would you be able to help move that along?"

A new staff member can do their first book-in correctly without anyone showing them the Salesforce field order or the description rules. That knowledge is just built in.
