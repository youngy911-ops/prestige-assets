Demo Script — AI Asset Book-In

Slattery Auctions · Keep under 12 minutes · Demo on phone, script on paper
Opening
~30 seconds
"I want to show you something I've built. Right now, booking in an asset takes 5–15 minutes — looking up specs on RitchieSpecs, typing every Salesforce field in the right order, writing the description from scratch following our format rules. Multiply that across a full sale and it adds up fast. I've built something that cuts that down to about 2 minutes. Let me just show you."
Live Demo
~8 minutes
"This is what it looks like. I pick the asset type — say Truck, Tipper. I upload my build plate photos from the camera roll. The AI runs for about 15 seconds..."
⏳ Wait for extraction to complete
"Here are the extracted fields — make, model, VIN from the plate, year, engine details. The ones in yellow are fields it's less confident about — I can fix those. Everything else it's pulled straight from the photo."
✏️ Fix a flagged field if needed, then tap Confirm
"Now I hit Generate Description..."
⏳ Wait for description
"Tipper format, correct footer, no dot points — it knows our rules by construction, not by chance. One tap to copy, paste straight into Salesforce. That's the whole process."
Who Can Use It
~1 minute
"This isn't just for me. Anyone can use it — valuations team can book in on-site before they leave the yard, BDMs can do it themselves without handing off to admin, new staff get it right on day one without knowing the Salesforce field order. For on-site auctions you walk the yard and you're done before you leave. Bulk general goods, large lots — instead of typing individual descriptions for 40 items, you move through them on your phone. The knowledge is in the app."
The Ask
~1 minute
"It covers all 8 asset types, every Salesforce subtype. The next step that makes it really powerful is Salesforce API access — instead of copy-paste, the app pushes directly into the record. I've built everything on my end. I just need IT to approve a Connected App. That's the ask — can you help move that along?"
💡 Tip: Don't rush the AI wait — it shows real work happening. Let them watch it think.
