Speaker Script

Asset Book-In App · Slattery Auctions presentation · Keep this open on your phone or a second screen

1
Title
~30 sec

"Thanks for the time. I want to show you something I've built that I think changes how we do asset book-ins. It's live, it's working, and I've been using it on real jobs."

"I'll keep this short — a few slides, then I'd rather just show you the actual tool."

2
The Problem
~1 min

"Here's what currently happens when we book in an excavator or a prime mover. We take photos on-site — that part's fine. But then we're manually cross-referencing websites to get the right specs, finding the exact Salesforce field order, and writing a description that has to follow our formatting rules exactly."

"That's around 10 minutes per asset, every single asset. Multiply that across a full sale — 50, 100 lots — and it adds up fast. And the consistency isn't always there either, especially across different staff or asset types they don't book in often."

3
The Solution
~1 min

"The app flips that process. Staff upload the build plate photos directly from their phone. The AI reads the plate and extracts every relevant Salesforce field — VIN, make, model, year, engine details, weight ratings, hours — and it uses its own knowledge to fill in spec fields it can infer from the model."

"The staff member reviews the AI's work, fixes anything wrong, and hits confirm. They get a ready-to-paste Salesforce fields block and a correctly formatted description. The whole thing takes about two minutes today, and under a minute once we have API access."

4
Live Demo
~3–4 min
⚡ Open the app before this slide. Have assets ready.

"Let me just show you. [Open app] This is what staff see — they pick the asset type, upload their photos, and the AI runs. [Wait ~15 sec] Here are the extracted fields. The ones highlighted are fields it's less confident about — we review those. [Fix one if needed]"

"Here's the Salesforce fields output, ready to paste. And then [tap Generate Description] here's the description — correct format for the subtype, correct footer. [Show copy button] That's it."

🔁 If the live demo fails, switch to backup video immediately — don't troubleshoot in the room.
5
Time Savings
~1 min

"The numbers are straightforward. We're at about 10 minutes per asset today. The app with copy-paste brings that to two minutes now. With the API integration, it's one minute."

"At 100 assets a week, that's about 780 hours a year freed up. That's nearly half a full-time headcount that could be doing inspections, yard work, or processing more volume — not typing specs into Salesforce."

6
ROI
~1 min

"The cost to run this is under $900 a year — that's AI fees, hosting, domain. All in."

"At a yardhand's fully loaded rate — $62k salary with super and on-costs works out to about $40 an hour — 780 hours freed up is worth around $31,000 a year. That's a 36-to-1 return on running costs. And that's just at 100 assets a week; it scales linearly from there."

7
Business Value
~45 sec

"Beyond the hours, there are a few things that are harder to put a number on. Anyone can do a correct book-in, first time — a new staff member doesn't need to know the Salesforce field order or the description rules. That knowledge is just built in."

"On-site auctions are a big one too. Right now there's a lag — photos on site, data entry later. With this, you walk the yard and you're done before you leave."

8

~1 min

"I can build that side of it. I just need IT to approve it and provision a sandbox. What I'm asking today is for your support to make that happen."

💬 Pause here. Let them respond before moving on.
9
Risk & Governance
~1 min

"Every write to Salesforce goes through the user's own login — there's a full audit trail, it's not a service account writing on behalf of everyone."

"And if the AI gets something wrong — the review step catches it. Nothing writes to Salesforce until a person has confirmed it."

✅ End here. Invite questions — don't rush to fill silence.
11
The Bigger Picture
~1 min

"The book-in app is what I'm asking you to approve today — but I want to be clear about what we're actually building here. The External Client App that IT provisions isn't just for book-ins. It's the authentication layer for AI automation across Salesforce."

"Once it's in place, the same foundation can power auction creation — AI reads a sale brief and builds the full Salesforce structure in seconds instead of an admin spending an hour on it. Consignor updates sent automatically when an asset status changes. Settlement statements generated and attached post-sale without anyone touching them. Field staff recording voice notes on site and having them populate Salesforce fields automatically."

"We approve this once, we build it once — and every future workflow that needs to talk to Salesforce uses it. The book-in app is the proof of concept. This is what it leads to."

💬 Let this land. Good place to invite questions about future potential before wrapping up.
Probable Q&A
"What if the AI gets something wrong?"
Every extraction is reviewed by a human before it touches Salesforce. The app shows what was extracted vs what was inferred — reviewer knows exactly what to double-check. Actually more reliable than manual entry, where a mistyped spec never gets flagged.
"Why not buy an off-the-shelf tool?"
Nothing on the market is built around Slattery's Salesforce schema and description conventions. Anything we bought would still need custom integration work, plus ongoing subscription costs. This is built around how Slattery actually works.
"What if you leave?"
Part of what the pilot decision covers. If it proves value, it becomes a supported internal tool — documented, handed over, or rebuilt by IT. Today it's a proof of concept; the pilot is how we decide whether to formalise it.
"Our data leaving the country concerns me."
Photos are hosted in Sydney. The AI call goes to OpenAI, but AU data residency is available now — images can be processed and stored at-rest in Australia. Zero Data Retention agreements are also available if needed. Happy to walk IT through the options.
"How much does this cost to run?"
About $0.02–$0.05 per asset in AI fees. At 100 a week that's $2–$5 a week. Total annual cost including hosting is under $900. Scales linearly so even at 10× volume it stays trivial.
"What does IT actually have to do?"
Create an External Client App in a sandbox, issue a Consumer Key and Secret, grant sandbox access, and set up a Permission Set for the pilot users. Everything else is on me. Production isn't touched until after the pilot review.
